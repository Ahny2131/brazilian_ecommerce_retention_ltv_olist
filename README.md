#Brazilian E-commerce Customer Retention Analysis

#Executive Summary

This project analyzes customer purchasing behavior in the Olist Brazilian E-Commerce dataset to understand how customer retention affects revenue generation.

The analysis reveals that customer retention is extremely low: **94% of customers make only one purchase**, and only **5.9% return for additional purchases**. Despite this, repeat customers generate significantly higher lifetime value, indicating a strong opportunity to increase revenue through improved retention.

Key findings:

- **94% of customers are one-time buyers**
- Repeat customers generate ~1.5× higher median lifetime value
- **50% of repeat purchases occur within 28 days**, indicating a critical early retention window
- Over 9**0% of revenue comes from one-time customers**, suggesting heavy dependence on customer acquisition

These findings suggest that improving early post-purchase engagement could significantly increase customer lifetime value and reduce reliance on new customer acquisition.

#Dataset

**Source**
Brazilian E-Commerce Public Dataset by Olist
https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/olistbr/brazilian-ecommerce

**Tables used **
The analysis focuses on the core transactional tables:

- olist_orders_dataset
- olist_customers_dataset
- olist_order_payments_dataset
**Time range**
Transactions span from 2016 to 2018, covering approximately two years of marketplace activity.

**Notes**
- The dataset includes orders with different statuses such as delivered, canceled, and unavailable.
- Only valid completed transactions are used for retention and revenue analysis.
- Data cleaning and filtering steps are documented in the notebook.

#Methodology

The analysis follows a structured workflow designed to evaluate customer retention and lifetime value.

##1. Data Preparation
Data from multiple tables were joined using order and customer identifiers to construct a customer-level purchase history.
Key steps included:

- filtering valid completed orders
- calculating order revenue using payment data
- identifying each customer's first purchase date
- aggregating purchase counts and revenue per customer

##2. Cohort Analysis
Customers were grouped by the month of their first purchase to analyze retention behavior over time.
Cohort analysis helps identify whether newer customer cohorts behave differently from earlier cohorts.

##3. Retention Analysis
Monthly retention rates were calculated to measure how many customers return after their first purchase.
Both retention heatmaps and retention curves were used to visualize customer drop-off patterns.

##4. Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)
Customer lifetime value was calculated as the total revenue generated by each customer across all purchases.
Because revenue distribution is highly skewed, a log transformation was applied to better visualize the long-tail distribution.

##5. Repeat Purchase Behavior
Customers were segmented into:
- one-time customers
- repeat customers

This allowed evaluation of:
- revenue contribution by segment
- revenue growth as purchase count increases
- distribution of repeat purchases.

##6. Time to Second Purchase
For customers with multiple purchases, the time between the first and second purchase was calculated.
This analysis helps identify the time window where retention strategies may be most effective.
