# Brazilian E-commerce Customer Retention Analysis

# Executive Summary

This project analyzes customer purchasing behavior in the Olist Brazilian E-Commerce dataset to understand how customer retention affects revenue generation.

The analysis reveals that customer retention is extremely low: **94% of customers make only one purchase**, and only **5.9% return for additional purchases**. Despite this, repeat customers generate significantly higher lifetime value, indicating a strong opportunity to increase revenue through improved retention.

**Key findings:**
- **94% of customers are one-time buyers**
- Repeat customers generate ~1.5× higher median lifetime value
- **50% of repeat purchases occur within 28 days**, indicating a critical early retention window
- Over 9**0% of revenue comes from one-time customers**, suggesting heavy dependence on customer acquisition

**Key metrics:**
- Customer retention rate (1st month): <1%
- Repeat customer rate: 5.9%
- Median time to second purchase: 28 days
- Revenue share from repeat customers: 8.5%


These findings suggest that improving early post-purchase engagement could significantly increase customer lifetime value and reduce reliance on new customer acquisition.

#Methodology

The analysis follows a structured workflow designed to evaluate customer retention and lifetime value.

## 1. Data Preparation
Data from multiple tables were joined using order and customer identifiers to construct a customer-level purchase history.
Key steps included:

- filtering valid completed orders
- calculating order revenue using payment data
- identifying each customer's first purchase date
- aggregating purchase counts and revenue per customer

## 2. Cohort Analysis
Customers were grouped by the month of their first purchase to analyze retention behavior over time.
Cohort analysis helps identify whether newer customer cohorts behave differently from earlier cohorts.

## 3. Retention Analysis
Monthly retention rates were calculated to measure how many customers return after their first purchase.
Both retention heatmaps and retention curves were used to visualize customer drop-off patterns.

## 4. Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)
Customer lifetime value was calculated as the total revenue generated by each customer across all purchases.
Because revenue distribution is highly skewed, a log transformation was applied to better visualize the long-tail distribution.

## 5. Repeat Purchase Behavior
Customers were segmented into:
- one-time customers
- repeat customers

This allowed evaluation of:
- revenue contribution by segment
- revenue growth as purchase count increases
- distribution of repeat purchases.

## 6. Time to Second Purchase
For customers with multiple purchases, the time between the first and second purchase was calculated.
This analysis helps identify the time window where retention strategies may be most effective.

#Full Analysis: Brazilian E-Commerce Customer Retention Analysis

## Problem

Customer retention is a key driver of sustainable growth in e-commerce businesses.
While acquiring new customers increases gross merchandise volume, long-term profitability often depends on the ability to convert first-time buyers into repeat customers.

This project analyzes customer purchasing behavior in the Olist Brazilian E-commerce dataset to answer the following questions:
- How many customers return after their first purchase?
- How much revenue is generated by repeat customers?
- How long does it take for customers to make a second purchase?
- How does repeat purchasing impact customer lifetime value (LTV)?
- The objective is to identify opportunities to improve customer retention and lifetime value.

## Analytical Approach

The analysis was conducted in Python using a combination of cohort analysis, retention analysis, and customer lifetime value modeling.

**Key analytical steps included:**
1. Building customer-level purchase histories
2. Identifying repeat purchasing behavior
3. Measuring customer lifetime value
4. Evaluating time between purchases
5. Quantifying revenue contribution by customer segment

**Tools used:**
- Python: Jupyter / Colab
- SQL: DuckDB 
- Libarry: Pandas | Matplotlib | Seaborn

## Analysis
### Cohort Analysis
Customers were grouped by the month of their first purchase to analyze retention behavior across cohorts.
Customer acquisition grew significantly from 2016 to late 2017, reflecting rapid marketplace expansion during this period.

### Customer Retention
Retention measures the percentage of customers who return after their first purchase.
Retention drops sharply after the first purchase, indicating that the majority of customers do not return to the platform.
This suggests the marketplace behaves more like a transactional marketplace rather than a loyalty-driven retail platform.

### Customer Lifetime Value Distribution
Customer revenue distribution is highly skewed.
Most customers generate relatively small lifetime value, while a small minority of customers generate significantly higher revenue.
A log transformation is used to better visualize this long-tail distribution.

### Revenue Contribution by Customer Type

Customers were segmented into two groups:
- One-time customers: 94.1%
- Repeat customers: 5.9%

Revenue contribution:
- One-time customers: 91.5%
- Repeat customers: 8.5%

Despite the higher spending potential of repeat customers, their contribution to total revenue remains limited due to their low proportion.

### Time to Second Purchase

**Key statistics:**
Median time to second purchase: 28 days
75% of second purchases occur within 126 days

This suggests that when customers do return, they tend to do so relatively soon after the first purchase.

##  Key Insights
### Extremely Low Customer Retention
Approximately **94% of customers make only one purchase**, indicating very low customer retention.

### Repeat Customers Have Higher Value
Customers who make a second purchase generate approximately **1.5X higher** median lifetime value compared with one-time buyers.

### Early Retention Window
Half of repeat purchases occur within **28 days after the first purchase**, indicating a critical retention window shortly after the initial transaction.

##  Business Impact

The platform currently relies heavily on **new customer acquisition**, as over **90% of revenue originates from one-time buyers.**

This model presents several risks:

- Higher customer acquisition costs
- Lower long-term revenue predictability
- Limited customer lifetime value growth

However, the analysis also reveals a significant opportunity:

Even modest improvements in repeat purchase rates could meaningfully increase customer lifetime value without proportionally increasing acquisition spending.

##  Recommendations
### Target the Early Retention Window

Since many repeat purchases occur within **28 days**, retention initiatives should focus on the post-purchase period.
Potential actions include:
- Post-purchase email campaigns
- Personalized product recommendations
- Follow-up promotions within the first month

### Incentivize the Second Purchase
Encouraging the second purchase can significantly increase customer lifetime value.

Examples:

- Second-purchase discount campaigns
- Loyalty programs
- Bundled product offers

## Conclusion

This analysis highlights that while the marketplace has experienced strong customer acquisition growth, customer retention remains extremely low.

Improving early retention and encouraging second purchases represents a key opportunity to increase customer lifetime value and improve the sustainability of the platform's revenue model.
